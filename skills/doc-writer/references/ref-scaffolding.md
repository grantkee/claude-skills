# Scaffolding pass

Used by the scaffolding agent (Wave 3). Read `tech-doc-rules.md` first.

Your sole job: cut filler and structural padding using the six rules below. Do not make any other changes.

## 1. Filler phrases

These are verbal throat-clearing. The sentence works without them.

| Filler | Replacement |
|---|---|
| in order to | to |
| due to the fact that | because |
| at this point in time | now |
| in the event that | if |
| has the ability to | can |
| it is important to note that | (delete, then state the thing) |
| it should be noted that | (delete) |
| on a daily basis | daily |
| a large number of | many |
| in the context of | in, for, during |
| with respect to / with regard to | about, for |
| prior to | before |
| subsequent to | after |
| in conjunction with | with |

## 2. Hedging

Pick one level of uncertainty and commit. Do not stack qualifiers.

Bad: "This could potentially lead to issues that might affect performance."
Good: "This may hurt performance."

If you are uncertain, say so directly: "It is unclear whether X causes Y." That is more honest than burying the uncertainty in four hedging words.

## 3. No generic positive conclusions

Do not end sections or documents with vague optimism. End with a fact, a next step, or nothing.

Bad: "The future of decentralized finance looks incredibly promising."
Bad: "This represents a major step in the right direction."
Good: "The next step is integrating the oracle price feed."
Good: (Just stop when you have made your point.)

## 4. No formulaic "challenges and future" sections

Do not append a "Challenges" or "Future Outlook" section unless it was specifically requested or the challenges are genuinely important to the reader's understanding. If there are real challenges worth mentioning, weave them into the main text where they are relevant, with specifics.

## 5. No false ranges

Do not use "from X to Y" when X and Y are not on a meaningful continuum.

Bad: "The library handles everything from authentication to caching."
Good: "The library handles authentication, rate limiting, and caching."

## 6. Hyphenated compounds

Common compound modifiers that readers understand without hyphens do not need them. AI text hyphenates them with perfect consistency, which is itself a tell.

Fine without hyphens in most contexts: real time, open source, end to end, high quality, long term, cross platform, data driven.

Still hyphenate when ambiguity would result or for less common compounds.
